Nine chapters. From foundations to leadership. Each chapter poses a question, gives you a framework, and lets you decide.

No hype. No buzzwords. Just what you need to understand, evaluate, and shape AI products.

## Learning Paths

Three tracks — depending on where you are.

| Track | Chapters | Who it's for |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Foundations Track** | 1–3 | PMs new to AI. Basics, landscape, strategy. |
| **Builder Track** | 4–6 | PMs actively building AI features. Technical decisions, evaluation, agents. |
| **Leader Track** | 7–9 | Senior PMs and managers. Ethics, execution, organization. |

All three tracks end with the **Capstone Project** — so you apply what you learned to a real-world case.

## Where Do You Start?

Five questions. Answer honestly and you'll know where to jump in.

1. **Do you know what a token is and how temperature affects output?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 2.

2. **Can you weigh build vs. buy vs. blend for AI components?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 4.

3. **Are you familiar with RAG, fine-tuning, and prompt engineering at a PM level?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 5.

4. **Have you already evaluated or shipped an AI feature?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 7.

5. **All no?**
   → Start at Chapter 1. No disadvantage — the curriculum builds on itself.
