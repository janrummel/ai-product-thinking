## Three learning tracks

| Track | Chapters | Who it's for |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Foundations** | 1–3 | PMs new to AI. Basics, landscape, strategy. |
| **Builder** | 4–6 | PMs actively building AI features. Technical decisions, evaluation, agents. |
| **Leader** | 7–9 | Senior PMs and managers. Ethics, execution, organization. |

All tracks end with a **Capstone Project** — apply what you learned to a real-world case.

## Who is this for?

- **Product Managers** who own or want to own AI features
- **Tech Leads** who want to understand the PM perspective on AI
- **Founders** building AI products
- **Anyone** who makes AI decisions — not just writes AI code

No ML background required. Technical concepts are explained at PM level: deep enough to decide, not deep enough to implement.

**Case Studies:** Notion AI, GitHub Copilot, Clearview AI, Duolingo Max.

## Where do you start?

Five questions. Answer honestly and you'll know where to jump in.

1. **Do you know what a token is and how temperature affects output?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 2.

2. **Can you weigh build vs. buy vs. blend for AI components?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 4.

3. **Are you familiar with RAG, fine-tuning, and prompt engineering at a PM level?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 5.

4. **Have you already evaluated or shipped an AI feature?**
   Yes → Start at Chapter 7.

5. **All no?**
   → Start at Chapter 1. No disadvantage — the curriculum builds on itself.

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>Do I need a technical background?</summary>

No. This curriculum is designed for PMs and decision-makers. Technical concepts like RAG, fine-tuning, and model selection are explained at PM level — deep enough to make informed decisions, not deep enough to implement them yourself.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How is this different from paid AI PM courses?</summary>

Most courses collect knowledge. This one trains decisions. Every lesson ends with a scenario where you make a call with real numbers and real constraints. No certificates, no fluff — just skills you can use Monday morning.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How long does it take?</summary>

Each chapter takes 2-4 hours. The full curriculum (9 chapters + capstone) takes about 30-40 hours. You can complete it in 2-3 months at a comfortable pace, or faster if you focus on one track.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I skip chapters?</summary>

Yes. Use the self-assessment above to find your starting point. Each chapter builds on previous ones, but the tracks (Foundations, Builder, Leader) work as entry points. If you already ship AI features, start at the Leader track.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Is this available in German?</summary>

Yes. The full curriculum is bilingual — every lesson exists in both English and German. Use the language switcher in the top navigation to switch anytime.
</details>
